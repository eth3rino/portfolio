# Custom mechanical keyboard

A fully custom mechanical keyboard — designed from scratch, not a kit. The goal was something that fits exactly how I work: a layout with a function row, a scroll wheel on the side for volume or timeline scrubbing, and dedicated media controls. Nothing off-the-shelf comes close to this combination, so building it from scratch is the only option.

## Motivation

Most off-the-shelf keyboards are a compromise. You either get good build quality with someone else's layout decisions, or full customisation with questionable construction. Building from scratch means neither concession — every choice is deliberate, from the switch actuation force to the layer structure in firmware.

The inspiration was seeing fully custom builds where the keyboard becomes a personal tool rather than a commodity peripheral — designed around your hands, your workflow, your aesthetic.

## Design goals

- **75% layout** — function row, arrow keys, no numpad. Practical without being oversized.
- **Scroll wheel on the side** — for volume, timeline scrubbing, or whatever the active layer maps it to.
- **Media control buttons** — play, skip, previous. Dedicated keys, no fn combos.
- **Blank keycaps** — no legends. Forces muscle memory, looks clean.
- **Custom firmware** — layers for symbols, macros for common sequences, full control over every key's behaviour including the scroll wheel and media keys.
- **Low profile** — thinner switches, lower case height. Easier on the wrists over long sessions.

## Status

Planned — the design is being refined, parts are being sourced. The case will be 3D printed in-house. Firmware will be ZMK or QMK depending on the controller choice.
